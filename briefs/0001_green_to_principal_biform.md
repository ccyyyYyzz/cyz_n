# Brief 0001 — Finite Green response to a unique principal biform

Status: active

## Role

Act as the originating mathematical physicist. Read
state/PROJECT_STATE.md and responses/0000_initial_architecture.md first.
Do not review the proposal; solve or reformulate its hardest remaining lemma.

## Exact bottleneck

The response algebra \(\mathcal A^\infty\), its physical regular character
germs and multiplication operators \(M_a\) are declared within a bounded event
class. Active linear response supplies only finite interval constraints on a
Green operator \(G\) between registered source and readout modules.

We propose to quantify over every bounded candidate local inverse/generator
\(L\) satisfying the data and

\[
L\in\operatorname{Diff}^{\le2}_{\mathcal A^\infty}.
\]

Each candidate gives

\[
\Gamma_L(a,b)=\frac12
\bigl(L(ab)-aL(b)-bL(a)+abL(1)\bigr)
\]

and hence a principal biform \(Q_L\) on
\(\Omega^1_{\mathcal A^\infty}\).

The needed theorem is not uniqueness of all lower-order coefficients. It is
that every data-consistent \(L\) induces the same conformal \(Q_L\), or that the
procedure correctly returns inconclusive.

## Required output

1. Choose the smallest finite-dimensional/truncated model in which the problem
   is nontrivial but exactly solvable.
2. Define raw registered experiments that implement enough source superposition,
   readout pairing and response-function gating to constrain \(L\), without
   assuming target-space localized source functions.
3. State necessary and sufficient linear-algebraic conditions for the finite
   Green data to identify the principal biform modulo conformal scale while
   leaving lower-order terms or gauge nonunique.
4. Express the condition invariantly under response-generator changes and
   source/readout basis changes.
5. Give an exact counterexample below the condition: two second-order
   data-consistent generators with non-conformal principal biforms.
6. Extend the statement from a scalar algebra module to the smallest
   multi-field case and say precisely when a universal cone is identifiable.
7. Provide a finite-noise margin and the certified / class-excluded /
   inconclusive rule.
8. State a seven-day kill test and the minimal simulation matrices.

## Prohibited shortcuts

- do not assume \(L\) is already known;
- do not feed coordinates, adjacency, a metric or localized target-space
  bump functions to the inference routine;
- do not identify inversion of one finite matrix representation of \(G\) with
  unique recovery of an unbounded differential operator;
- do not use wavefronts unless their localization is itself generated by the
  registered response algebra;
- do not let the response realization bound \(n_J\) stand in for the generator
  class bound \(B_L\).

## Decision criterion

If conformal \(Q_L\) is not identifiable even in the clean bounded toy, provide
the strongest exact impossibility theorem and identify the smallest additional
registered operation that repairs it.
