# Brief 0020 — unconditioned certified population law and anonymous dimension kernel

Baseline: the final verified Brief 0019 commit.

## Mission

Push the single-state certified event map through a preregistered,
rank-unconditioned finite-\(K\) source population.  Preserve source-invalid,
censored, exceptional and numerically unresolved mass.  The result must be a
joint probability law with finite-sample uncertainty and a permutation-
equivariant microscopic rate interface for later dimension dynamics.

This round is the first population calculation in the project.  It is not
allowed to identify the number of large target cycles with response-visible
dimension, and it is not allowed to call a three-ceiling a stable \(3+1\)
selection.

The authoritative outcomes of the round are:

1. a certified lower/upper probability ledger for every primary event class;
2. a cluster-preserving empirical law for
   \[
   (T,j,b,\ell,a,\sigma_3)
   \]
   with undefined and unresolved components represented explicitly;
3. a regulator- and horizon-sensitivity report;
4. an anonymous nine-cycle encounter/annihilation-kernel interface;
5. either a resolved rate separation suitable for the next dynamical gate, or
   a quantitative no-go/inconclusive statement.

## 1. Inherited non-negotiable boundary

Import, without weakening, the final Brief 0018 source registry and event
record contract and the final Brief 0019 certificate replayer.

Every source draw is counted exactly once.  The population pipeline may not:

- reject a draw because it is source-invalid;
- redraw after a missed encounter;
- condition on encounter rank, normal dimension or \(\sigma_3\);
- drop an unresolved proof box or exceptional event;
- replace a complete tie cluster by one lexicographic member;
- infer an infinite-time no-entry event from a finite observation window;
- use a downstream response winner, visible-rank label or reaction result as
  a sampler or solver input.

The physical source population and any variance-reduction proposal are
different measures.  Their identities, seeds and Radon--Nikodym derivatives
must be separately registered.

## 2. Frozen inference protocol

Before running the authoritative population:

1. freeze the source-cell family and the one canonical audit cell;
2. freeze the Fourier-cutoff grid, observation horizons and precision ladder;
3. freeze the inference sample counts or a valid confidence-sequence stopping
   rule;
4. freeze source, solver, bootstrap and audit substream derivations;
5. freeze all simultaneous confidence allocations;
6. freeze an operation and unresolved-mass budget;
7. publish a normalized-LF code inventory and semantic hashes.

A small pilot may be used only to estimate cost and choose the frozen
operation budget.  Pilot draws, seeds and candidate boxes are forbidden from
the inferential ledger.

The initial authoritative grid should include at least

\[
K\in\{1,2,4\}
\]

unless the validated source-validity predicate excludes one of these cells.
Larger \(K\) values may be added, but a failed or expensive cell is retained
as unresolved rather than removed from the reported grid.

Use at least three nested finite horizons

\[
0<T_1<T_2<T_3
\]

with a common source state and continuation convention.  A longer-horizon
record must refine, not contradict, the shorter-horizon record after
restriction to its window.

## 3. Population sample space

For draw \(q\), store:

- source registry and source-state semantic hashes;
- source validity vector;
- solver registry and proof-bundle hashes;
- one total event record;
- certificate-replayer verdict;
- operation counts and final precision;
- all primary and orthogonal exceptional flags;
- episode and complete-cluster records;
- rank object and its proof status;
- the normal-fibre object only when rank is certified;
- the complete unresolved manifest otherwise.

Define one categorical population variable

\[
C_q\in\mathcal C
\]

whose alphabet is the frozen primary outcome alphabet.  Orthogonal flags are
additional random variables and are not used to split or duplicate the unit
mass of \(C_q\).

For a finite tie cluster, the sample unit remains one pair-level event.  A
member-level measure may be reported only as a separately normalized Palm or
counting measure.  It may not silently replace the pair-level population.

## 4. Exact mass accounting with unresolved outcomes

For \(N\) independent source draws, let \(n_c\) be the number replay-certified
in class \(c\), and let \(u_c\) be the number of unresolved draws whose
manifest is logically compatible with \(c\).

The deterministic identification interval is

\[
\frac{n_c}{N}
\le p_c\le
\frac{n_c+u_c}{N}.
\]

Sampling uncertainty must then be added with one simultaneous multinomial
procedure fixed in advance.  Acceptable choices include:

- exact binomial Clopper--Pearson bounds with a frozen Bonferroni allocation;
- a simultaneous Sison--Glaz-type multinomial construction whose numerical
  implementation is independently checked;
- an anytime-valid categorical confidence sequence if adaptive stopping is
  used.

Do not print a point estimate without its identification interval and
sampling interval.  The total ledger must satisfy, pathwise,

\[
\sum_{c\in\mathcal C} n_c=N,
\]

and every lower/upper interval must be reproducible from integer counts.

`source_invalid`, `numerically_unresolved`, left censoring, right censoring,
rank-unresolved and certificate-replay failure each have visible mass.  A
certificate-replay failure is a pipeline failure class, not a physical event
class, and blocks publication of the affected physical row.

## 5. Holding time and censoring

For a certified first entry, \(T\) is the certified time interval of the
earliest complete physical cluster.  For a finite-window no-entry outcome,
record right censoring at the horizon.  For an initially active episode,
record left censoring and do not invent its entry time.

Publish:

- the cumulative incidence of every first-entry class;
- the survival interval for remaining armed without a certified entry;
- the mass of initially active and source-invalid states;
- the unresolved contribution at every horizon.

If interval-censored survival estimation is used, its likelihood and interval
algorithm must be preregistered.  Kaplan--Meier on arbitrarily selected
endpoints is forbidden.

Exact recurrence or global lower-bound `no_entry_proved` mass is shown
separately from finite-window right-censored no-entry mass.

## 6. Rank-blind joint bundle law

For every certified entry member, retain

\[
j=\operatorname{rank}J,\qquad
b=P_Ns,\qquad
\ell=s-b,
\]

the normal-frame-free normal object, the local response mark \(a\), and the
metric-normalized singular values of

\[
G^{1/2}JH^{-1/2}.
\]

Here \(a\) is a downstream registered mark and is not a substitute for
encounter rank \(j\).

The population law must contain:

1. the unconditional rank mixture, including no-entry and rank-unresolved
   atoms;
2. the event-conditioned rank mixture with its denominator printed;
3. the conditional law of \(b\) in each certified \(N_j\);
4. the distribution of \(\|b\|_G\) and directional invariants;
5. the complete cluster multiplicity and simultaneous-member law;
6. the distribution of \(\sigma_3\) on certified rank-three members;
7. an atom `not_defined_rank_below_3` rather than a fabricated zero
   \(\sigma_3\);
8. lower/upper small-\(\sigma_3\) tail bounds after unresolved mass is added.

For preregistered \(\varepsilon\) values, report

\[
P(\sigma_3\le\varepsilon,\ j=3,\ \text{entry})
\]

unconditionally and conditionally, with both denominators explicit.  Fit no
power law unless the fit range and loss were frozen independently of the
observed tail.  Compare the physical tail to the Brief 0017 Gaussian and
volume-Palm exponents only as falsifiable controls.

## 7. Rare-event calculation

The primary estimator is always the unconditioned direct source pushforward.
If its certified entry count is too small, a separate proposal law may be
used only when:

1. its support contains the full source support relevant to the claimed row;
2. the exact density ratio \(d\mu/dq\) is derived from the ambient source
   measure;
3. proposal selection uses no realized rank or event outcome;
4. all weights are serialized and independently replayed;
5. a bounded-weight or otherwise rigorous finite-sample interval is supplied;
6. the unweighted primary ledger remains visible.

Adaptive multilevel splitting may estimate a rare probability only through a
separately validated path-space identity.  Treating correlated descendants as
independent source draws is forbidden.

## 8. Regulator and source-family sensitivity

Changing \(K\) changes the finite-dimensional microcanonical measure.  It is
not a refinement of one sample unless an explicit coupling is defined.

For every \(K\), print:

- source-valid mass;
- each event-class mass interval;
- unresolved mass and operation distribution;
- rank mixture and small-\(\sigma_3\) tail;
- energy partition across Fourier bands;
- \(k_{\max}\ell_s\) and graph-validity margins.

At least one comparison must hold the registered physical energy and torus
cell fixed.  A second comparison may hold energy per active mode fixed, but it
is a different source family and must be named.

No continuum statement is allowed from three \(K\) values.  The permitted
statements are finite-regulator stability, detected drift, or unresolved
regulator dependence.

## 9. Anonymous anisotropic-torus kernel

The dimension-dynamics interface must not take a preferred number `3` or a
visible-rank label as input.  Its geometric state is the complete unordered
or \(S_9\)-equivariant vector of target-cycle lengths,

\[
\mathbf L=(L_1,\ldots,L_9),
\]

together with energy, winding populations, reservoir variables and history.

For each winding cycle \(A\), construct the same opposite-winding source and
event calculation after the exact axis permutation sending \(A\) to the
canonical solver axis.  Define a certified encounter kernel

\[
\mathcal K_A(dT,dj,db,d\ell,da,d\sigma_3
 \mid \mathbf L,E,\mathcal R,h).
\]

It must obey

\[
\mathcal K_{\pi(A)}
(\pi\cdot B\mid
 \pi\mathbf L,E,\pi\mathcal R,\pi h)
=
\mathcal K_A(B\mid
 \mathbf L,E,\mathcal R,h)
\]

for every tested \(\pi\in S_9\), up to only the declared statistical
uncertainty and unresolved mass.  Adjacent transpositions are sufficient for
the executable group test.

The effective suppression associated with several large cycles must be read
from the full radius-dependent kernel.  It may not be inserted as
\((\ell-3)_+\) or by selecting a \(d=3\) branch.

## 10. Reaction port and rate bounds

First entry is not annihilation.  Compose the certified encounter law with a
separately registered reaction probability

\[
\mathcal P_{\rm react}
(j,b,\ell,a,\sigma_3;g_s,\alpha',\ldots).
\]

If only bounds

\[
0\le P_-(\cdot)\le
\mathcal P_{\rm react}(\cdot)\le
P_+(\cdot)\le1
\]

are physically supported, propagate those bounds rather than choosing a
profile.

For a declared pair-renewal clock, define

\[
\lambda_A^- \le
\frac{\mathbb E[P_{\rm react}\,\mathbf 1_{\rm certified}]}
     {\mathbb E[T_{\rm cycle}]}
\le \lambda_A^+,
\]

with censoring and unresolved mass treated adversarially in the lower/upper
bounds.  The return/renewal clock is a separate source-to-return object; a
fresh independent impact redraw is not assumed unless derived.

Publish three layers separately:

1. certified geometric first-entry kernel;
2. reaction-profile or reaction-bound composition;
3. pair-density/return-clock conversion to a macroscopic rate.

The independent population replayer must reject or expose:

1. deletion of source-invalid rows before normalization;
2. rank-three conditioning of the source or solver;
3. replacement of a tie cluster by one representative;
4. an unresolved row relabelled as no entry;
5. finite-window censoring relabelled as infinite-time no entry;
6. mismatched source and solver hashes;
7. reuse of pilot draws in the inference ledger;
8. overlap of source, solver and bootstrap substreams;
9. an adaptive stop rule without a confidence sequence;
10. a second audit cell silently substituted for the canonical cell;
11. a proposal sample with missing support or an incorrect density ratio;
12. member-count weighting silently substituted for pair weighting;
13. a rank-below-three row assigned \(\sigma_3=0\);
14. removal of a high-\(K\) invalid or unresolved cell;
15. a hidden preferred-three field, branch or axis ordering;
16. failure of an adjacent-\(S_9\) equivariance replay;
17. reaction data used by the geometric event solver;
18. a point rate whose uncertainty or unresolved interval is omitted.

Each mutant must fail at its intended gate.

## 13. Acceptance

This round passes only when:

- every preregistered source draw has exactly one replayed total outcome;
- all integer ledgers close without filtered mass;
- simultaneous probability intervals are independently reproduced;
- Windows and Linux agree on semantic manifests;
- the production source and solver hashes match the frozen upstream
  artifacts;
- all exceptional, censored and unresolved mass remains visible;
- the radius-dependent cycle kernel passes adjacent-\(S_9\) equivariance;
- the conclusion is unchanged when unresolved mass is assigned
  adversarially, or the conclusion is explicitly downgraded.

The permitted success statement is:

> For the declared finite-\(K\), finite-horizon source family, the certified
> event map has been pushed forward without rank conditioning or missing
> mass, yielding probability bounds for the joint near-encounter law and an
> anonymous radius-dependent microscopic rate interface.

It is not:

> Three spatial dimensions or \(3+1\) spacetime have been dynamically
> selected.
