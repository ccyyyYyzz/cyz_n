# Decision 0010 — adopt the all-rank problem and local underdetermination, not the claimed sharp winner no-go

## Verdict

Response 0010 identifies the correct next object: one exchangeable law, one
generator, one response construction, and a simultaneous comparison of all ten
visible-rank outputs.  Its physical diagnosis is retained:

> the currently authorized isotropic source data do not determine the
> anisotropic recollision kernel or the initial fluctuation law needed to
> calculate an all-rank residence ordering.

The response does **not** yet prove its stronger headline claim that every rank
can be made the unique winner in the declared source-compatible fiber, nor the
corollary that the three-margin changes sign over that fiber.  It is therefore
adopted as a repaired conditional scaffold plus a local score-underdetermination
result, not as a completed sharp all-rank no-go theorem.

## Adopted results

1. The two-stage continuous recollapse comparison is correct.  For
   \(h\ge0\) the \(\underline u\) endpoint applies; after \(h\le0\), the
   \(\bar u\) endpoint gives a forward negative region and a finite recollapse
   bound.
2. The conditional construction is genuinely arbitrary-cardinality:
   \(m+1\to m\) for \(m=0,\ldots,8\).  Its \(m=3\) case is a corollary, not a
   selection theorem.
3. A full-horizon Poisson cap per trapped direction is the correct way to
   avoid accumulated-loss errors.  Clearance, reverse creation, cemetery
   exits, deterministic response error, and statistical coverage must remain
   separately named.
4. The all-rank quantities \(I_m,H_m,R_m,L_m\) are the correct comparison
   architecture.  Initial mass, dynamic arrival, time-window occupancy, and
   no-exit leakage are distinct observables.
5. The equal-hazard binomial calculation is an exact abstract negative
   control: exchangeability permits every rank to be the mode on some open
   rate-ratio cell.  Symmetry removes axis names but does not fix a
   cardinality.
6. At a fixed, label-stable response partition \(P_k\), the Duhamel derivative
   is correct.  A nonzero pairing proves that the exact score \(R_k\) is not
   identified by the restricted source data.
7. A complete anisotropic recollision-time/impact-mark law and an independently
   declared exchangeable initial law are the right next physical objects to
   derive or measure.  Their provenance must precede the response classifier
   and the all-rank result.

## Claims not adopted

### 1. Score variation is not winner variation

The condition

\[
D R_k(Q)[\Delta Q]\ne0
\]

shows that \(R_k\) varies.  It does not show that

\[
\operatorname*{argmax}_m R_m
\]

changes, or that an existing positive three-margin crosses zero.  The global
claim needs either two explicit source-compatible completions with opposite
margin signs, a certified path crossing the winner boundary, or a stronger
finite-horizon controllability theorem that is actually proved for the
declared completion fiber.

### 2. The response cells vary with the generator

The response partition is constructed from poles, residues and the anonymous
Green/relation replay.  Thus \(P_k=P_k(Q)\).  The displayed derivative omits
the classifier variation.  It is exact only inside a predeclared open region
where every response margin keeps the labels fixed.  Global completions must
rerun the classifier rather than reuse a frozen \(P_k\).

### 3. The finite and continuum transition statements are different

The velocity comparison is a continuum ODE theorem.  The upwind geometry
process is a stochastic CTMC approximation and has a nonzero probability of
making too few geometry jumps before the deadline.  A finite-chain transition
claim therefore needs an exact geometry hitting probability from the same
\(Q\), or a validated semigroup-to-event enclosure.  Deterministic truncation
error alone is not a pathwise probability bound.

### 4. The proposed finite operator is not yet fully closed

- The residual update need not land in the declared finite set
  \(\mathcal R_{\rm num}\); an exact closure, bin enclosure, jump-budget
  construction, or cemetery rule is missing.
- With the row-generator convention, the column probability channel of the
  augmented operator carries \(Q^T\), not literally \(Q\).  Reset-map indices
  must be oriented explicitly.
- The finite matrices \(A_x,R_{xy},B,C,M\), their covariance/normalization
  rules, and a noncircular local response-cell construction remain declared
  architecture rather than a replayable artifact.
- Worst leakage is undefined for an empty rank cell and must return an explicit
  undefined status.

### 5. The all-or-nothing pathwise control is overstated

An exchangeable law remains exchangeable under an equivariant generator, but
independent directional jumps do not preserve pointwise equality of all nine
directions.  Synchronous band crossing is exact only for the deterministic
diagonal mean flow or for a separately declared synchronized-jump control.

### 6. The orbit-rate ledger is sufficient, not proved smallest

Specifying every reachable orbit weight and edge rate is sufficient to fix a
finite chain.  Nonzero score derivatives do not prove that no smaller lumped
generator, committor, or certified score statistic determines the winner.
The continuum covariance \(\Sigma^\perp\) also does not by itself determine
absolute recollision frequency, non-Gaussian tails, history correlations,
reverse channels, or the initial law.

Moreover, \(S_9\)-invariance alone does not prevent hidden cardinality coding:
"exactly three clear directions" is itself an \(S_9\)-invariant property.  A
candidate kernel is target-neutral only when its constitutive variables and
data provenance are fixed before the response cells and contain neither a
visible count nor a proxy for it.
