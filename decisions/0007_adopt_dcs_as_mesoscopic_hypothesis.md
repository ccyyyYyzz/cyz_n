## Not adopted

The current DCS construction has not yet proved that its rank selection is
inequivalent to a target controller. For any chosen integer (T), the input

\[
c_T(s)=1_{s\le T}
\]

combined with release sampling (c(s+1)) and reconfinement sampling
(1-c(s)) reproduces exactly “up below (T), down above (T)”. The source
fixed-impact rate is finite and positive and does not itself derive this step.

Five further corrections are mandatory:

1. absolute event attempt frequency must enter rank flux; rescaling both race
   hazards currently changes the proposed departure pole but not attraction
   time;
2. the deterministic two-guard PDMP and the Poisson birth-death chain are not
   equivalent when both clocks are active;
3. homofission reactions such as (2\rightleftarrows1+1) require falling
   factorial/symmetry propensities, so the displayed product-Poisson proof is
   false as written;
4. the full target Lyapunov theorem controls no residual for signature, time,
   regularity, overlap or scale and has a zero-(V) off-target counterexample;
5. (-\Omega) is not generally a pole of the coupled transfer; full coupled
   eigenvalues and nonzero observable residues are required.

The durable result is therefore a source-anchored DCS research hypothesis and
a precise list of no-go gates—not yet the physical explanation of (3+1).
