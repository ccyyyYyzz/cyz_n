## Not adopted as physics

WIRRC is presently a parameterized hybrid control model, not a microscopic
selection theory. Its rule explicitly performs a rank move toward

\[
s_p=2p+1.
\]

No action, Hamiltonian or normalized stochastic generator derives those
upward and downward rates. The model also supplies the defect dimension
\(p=1\), an index-one hyperbolic sector, the response-consensus law and a
complete readout. The \(p=2\) counterfactual is a valuable arithmetic
non-encoding test, but it does not explain why stringlike defects dominate.

The full-readout choice \(R_\Pi=I\) proves zero kernel only on a quotient that
has already been selected. The nilpotent Green operator reads back a supplied
\(q\). Both remain exact unit controls rather than derivations of physical
visibility or propagation.

## Required theorem repairs

The selection theorem must use a nonempty microscopic target stratum defined
without the phrase "already has a certified plateau". Its basin must remain a
forward tube until hitting, and a hybrid proof must include complete
trajectories, piecewise absolute continuity and

\[
V(X^+)\le V(X^-)
\]

at every reset. The response-defect reset map across a change of rank must be
defined before the total Lyapunov claim is used.
