## Theorem 1 — presentation-orbit criterion

The interval-consistent candidates have one registered physical presentation

\[
\boxed{
\forall C,C'\in\mathfrak C_D,
\qquad
\operatorname{Iso}_{\rm pres}(C,C')\ne\varnothing.

\[

=
\left\{

\operatorname{Iso}_{\rm pres}(C,C')=\varnothing
\right\}
\tag{14}
\]

is empty.

Within the bounded polynomialized class, (14) is a finite first-order formula

### Proof

carrier, physical branch and refined cover with one registered reference
object.

Conversely, if all candidates represent one registered physical

# 4. Correct selected-singular-subspace theorem

A noisy matrix is generically full rank.  Its literal row space is therefore
usually the entire coefficient space and is not a relation estimator.

## Theorem 2 — uniform selected right-singular-subspace bound

Let \(M\in\mathbb R^{m\times n}\) be any exact candidate matrix with
class-fixed rank \(r\), and let \(\widehat M\) be the calibrated nominal matrix.
Assume

\[
\|M-\widehat M\|_2\le\eta
\tag{19}
\]

for every interval-consistent candidate.

Write

\[
\widehat\sigma_1\ge\cdots\ge\widehat\sigma_p
\]

for the singular values of \(\widehat M\), and let

\[
\widehat{\mathcal S}_r
=
\operatorname{span}
\{\widehat v_1,\ldots,\widehat v_r\}
\tag{20}
\]

be the **selected top-\(r\) right singular subspace**.

Define

\[
\varepsilon_A
=
(2\|\widehat M\|_2+\eta)\eta
\tag{21}
\]

and the squared spectral gap

\[
g_r^{(2)}
=
\widehat\sigma_r^2-\widehat\sigma_{r+1}^2.
\tag{22}
\]

If

\[
g_r^{(2)}>2\varepsilon_A,
\tag{23}
\]

then the exact row space

\[
\mathcal S(M)=\operatorname{row}M
\]

obeys the uniform Davis--Kahan bound

\[
\boxed{
\left\|
\sin\Theta(
\mathcal S(M),
\widehat{\mathcal S}_r
)
\right\|_2
\le
\frac{\varepsilon_A}
{g_r^{(2)}-\varepsilon_A}.
}
\tag{24}
\]

Consequently any two exact candidate row spaces satisfy the pairwise bound

\[
\left\|
\sin\Theta(
\mathcal S(M),\mathcal S(M')
)
\right\|_2
\le
2
\frac{\varepsilon_A}
{g_r^{(2)}-\varepsilon_A}.
\tag{25}
\]

### Proof

The row space of \(M\) is the top-\(r\) eigenspace of

\[
A=M^\top M.
\]

The selected nominal space is the top-\(r\) eigenspace of

\[
\widehat A=\widehat M^\top\widehat M.
\]

Using (19),

\[
\|A-\widehat A\|_2
\le
(\|M\|_2+\|\widehat M\|_2)\eta
\le
(2\|\widehat M\|_2+\eta)\eta
=
\varepsilon_A.
\]

Condition (23) keeps the selected eigenvalue cluster separated from the
nominal tail after perturbation.  Davis--Kahan gives (24).  The pairwise bound
follows through the common nominal subspace.

## Direct Wedin form

A convenient direct sufficient condition is

\[
\widehat\sigma_r-\widehat\sigma_{r+1}>2\eta.
\tag{26}
\]

Then Wedin's theorem gives the valid conservative bound

\[
\left\|
\sin\Theta(
\mathcal S(M),
\widehat{\mathcal S}_r
)
\right\|_2
\le
\min\!\left\{
1,\,
\frac{2\eta}
{\widehat\sigma_r-\widehat\sigma_{r+1}-\eta}
\right\}.
\tag{27}
\]

The exact numerical constant is less important than the logical requirements:

- \(r\) is fixed independently by the candidate class;
- the top-\(r\) right singular subspace is selected;
- the tail \(\widehat\sigma_{r+1}\) appears explicitly;
- all norms are calibrated;
- the full row space of a full-rank noisy matrix is never used.
