# Response 0006 — Scale functor, quotient-safe selection, and the worldvolume rank-release mechanism

## Executive decision

No published mechanism in the mandatory screen currently certifies the closed
operational chain

\[
\text{anonymous responses}
\longrightarrow
\text{visible branch and cotangent}
\longrightarrow
\text{one Lorentz common cone and time component}
\longrightarrow
\text{an attractive visible }3+1\text{ basin}
\longrightarrow
\text{a response-level departure}.
\]

The compact-radius/Kaluza--Klein route is an exact hiding and stability
control, not a selector.  Brandenberger--Vafa supplies the strongest explicit
three-versus-more spatial interaction seam, but only conditionally inside a
one-time, nine-torus, homogeneous string-gas setting and without the common
multi-probe cone.  Published graph, CDT, tensor and GFT models either encode
dimension through valence, rank or building blocks, or study dimensional flow
inside a fixed sector.  The 2026 Lorentzian IKKT phase is the strongest
pre-geometric reserve, but it is presently seeded, anisotropically deformed,
finite-\(N\), and of unresolved saddle weight; it has not supplied an open
basin or an anonymous common-cone response interface.

The primary next-cycle mechanism should therefore be a new model:

> **worldvolume-intersection rank release with response consensus
> (WIRRC).**

Its microscopic carrier contains every rank from zero to a declared finite
cutoff and no preferred spatial subset.  A \(p\)-dimensional defect has a
\((p+1)\)-dimensional history.  Two generic histories in \(s+1\) event
dimensions have expected direct-intersection dimension

\[
\delta_p(s)=2(p+1)-(s+1)=2p+1-s.
\]

The unique spatial rank at which a clean direct interaction is pointlike is

\[
s_p=2p+1.
\]

For stringlike defects, \(p=1\), this gives \(s_p=3\) and total visible
dimension \(s_p+1=4\).  The model does not contain the numerals three or four
in its state constraint, seed, projector rank or response dictionary; changing
the defect worldvolume changes the selected rank.  This is a genuine
non-encoding audit, but it also makes the unresolved physical gate explicit:
the model does not yet explain why the relevant defect sector is \(p=1\).

The response introduces three exact mathematical bridges.

1. **Scale-direction theorem.**  
   A nested intervention algebra gives a Kähler transitivity sequence.  Visible
   dimension is monotone only when the old cotangent map is injective.  Its
   kernel diagnoses collapse/hiding; relative Kähler differentials diagnose
   newly revealed directions.

2. **Gap-free plateau and selection theorem.**  
   A visible \(3+1\) plateau is a nonzero scale interval on which the repaired
   all-candidate certificate holds at every scale with one common positive
   margin vector and with full structure-preserving scale maps.  Genuine
   selection additionally requires a resolved non-\(3+1\) predecessor, a
   positive-radius basin, a finite-arrival Lyapunov budget, a same-family
   non-encoding proof, and a zero-kernel visibility proof.

3. **Finite-time rank-release theorem.**  
   A permutation/orthogonal-equivariant hybrid semigroup moves every resolved
   spatial rank toward \(s_p\).  With a progress clock \(c\), the exact
   Lyapunov function

   \[
   V_{\rm rank}=|s-s_p|-c
   \]

   decreases at a uniform rate and is continuous across rank-changing events.
   If

   \[
   V_{\max}\le\gamma_{\min}L_{\min},
   \]

   every trajectory in the open basin reaches the target rank before the
   available flow length \(L_{\min}\), and the target rank is forward
   invariant.

WIRRC closes the **dimension-variable quotient and attraction** seam in an
executable model.  It does not yet close the microscopic origin of the
one-time hyperbolic viability sector or derive multi-field response consensus
from a fundamental action.  Those remain explicit gates rather than being
hidden inside a fixed \(3+1\) probe kinetic term.

# 2. The scale-direction theorem

## 2.1 Relative Kähler sequence

For (1), the transitivity sequence of Kähler differentials is

\[
\mathcal A_{\lambda'}
\otimes_{\mathcal A_\lambda}
\Omega^1_{\mathcal A_\lambda/\mathbb R}
\xrightarrow{\alpha_{\lambda'\lambda}}
\Omega^1_{\mathcal A_{\lambda'}/\mathbb R}
\longrightarrow
\Omega^1_{\mathcal A_{\lambda'}/\mathcal A_\lambda}
\longrightarrow0.
\tag{6}
\]

Tensoring at the compatible physical character \(x'\) gives

\[
T_x^*B_\lambda
\xrightarrow{\alpha_{x';\lambda'\lambda}}
T_{x'}^*B_{\lambda'}
\longrightarrow
R_{x';\lambda'\lambda}
\longrightarrow0,
\tag{7}
\]

where

\[
R_{x';\lambda'\lambda}
=
\mathbb R_{x'}
\otimes_{\mathcal A_{\lambda'}}
\Omega^1_{\mathcal A_{\lambda'}/\mathcal A_\lambda}
\tag{8}
\]

is the relative visible cotangent space.

The first map in (7) need not be injective.  That is precisely the point at
which a coarse/fine comparison can collapse an old visible direction.

## Theorem 1 — preserved, revealed and collapsed directions

Assume \(B_\lambda\) and \(B_{\lambda'}\) are regular at \(x,x'\), with finite
cotangent dimensions.

1. If

   \[
   \ker\alpha_{x';\lambda'\lambda}=0,
   \tag{9}
   \]

   then

   \[
   d_{\lambda'}
   =
   d_\lambda
   +
   \dim R_{x';\lambda'\lambda}.
   \tag{10}
   \]

2. If (9) holds and \(R_{x';\lambda'\lambda}=0\), every old direction is
   preserved and no new direction is revealed; \(\alpha\) is an isomorphism.

3. If (9) holds and \(\dim R=r>0\), exactly \(r\) new response directions are
   revealed at the finer scale.

4. If \(\ker\alpha\ne0\), at least one old direction collapses.  No monotonic
   dimension statement is valid, and a lower measured dimension may be a
   quotient/hiding effect.

### Proof

Sequence (7) is right exact.  Under (9) it is short exact, so dimensions add.
The remaining statements are its zero-rank and nonzero-rank cases.  If (9)
fails, the dimension of the image is smaller than \(d_\lambda\), and no
addition formula with \(d_\lambda\) follows.

# 3. Plateau, hiding, stability and genuine selection

## 3.1 Gap-free visible \(3+1\) plateau

Let

\[
I=[\lambda_-,\lambda_+],
\qquad
\lambda_+>\lambda_-.
\]

1. for every \(\lambda\in I\), the raw fiber (3) is nonempty;

   set or a numerical tolerance filling an unproved gap;

   \[
   m_I
   =
   \inf_{\lambda\in I}

## 3.2 Three logically different phenomena

### Visibility or hiding

A fixed microscopic state can look four-dimensional at one scale because a
coarse response map has a nontrivial kernel or because relative directions
are not registered.  This is diagnosed by (7)--(12).  It is not dynamical
selection.

### Stability

A state already in the plateau stratum remains there under the dynamics.
Forward invariance is necessary but does not show attraction or selection.

### Selection

A same-family flow starts from an open set containing a **resolved**
non-\(3+1\) state and reaches the plateau stratum in a controlled flow length.
The predecessor must fail the target by a genuine response-level obstruction,
such as a certified branch dimension different from four.  An inconclusive
predecessor does not count.

## Theorem 2 — operational selection bridge

Assume \(\mathfrak M_{\mathscr D}\ne\varnothing\).  Suppose the following six
gates hold for every \(M\in\mathfrak M_{\mathscr D}\), with common positive
bounds, and suppose the resulting plateau packages occupy one registered
microscopic/full-response orbit.

### S2. Resolved predecessor

For every \(M\), there exists \(X_{-,M}\) in the flow direction and a scale

### S3. Positive-radius basin

For every \(M\), there is an open set
\(U_M\subset\mathscr X_M\), of a uniformly positive radius in the declared

### S4. Finite-arrival Lyapunov budget

For every \(M\), there is a continuous hybrid Lyapunov function
\(V_M:U_M\to[0,\infty)\) with

\[
V_M^{-1}(0)=U_M\cap\mathscr S_I(M),
\tag{15}

forward invariance of \(\mathscr S_I(M)\), and

\[
D^+V_M(\Phi_\tau^M X)
\le

\quad

\[

=
\sup_{M\in\mathfrak M_{\mathscr D}}
\sup_{X\in U_M}V_M(X)
\le
\gamma_{\min}L_{\min},
\tag{17}
\]

where \(L_{\min}\) is the shortest available flow interval.

### S5. Target non-encoding

The audit above is proved for the same dynamics family.

### S6. Positive visibility and non-hiding

For every state on every trajectory used in the claim:

- the actual event cotangent directions inject into the registered response

\[

\le

\tag{18}

\(\lambda\in I\), this certifies a same-family attractive, visible,

### Proof

For each \(M\), while \(V_M>0\), integration of (16) gives

\[
V_M(\Phi_\tau^M X)
\le
V_M(X)-\gamma_{\min}\tau.
\]

Therefore \(V_M\) reaches zero no later than (18).  Equation (17) places this
arrival within the available flow.  Forward invariance keeps the state in the
target stratum.  S1 supplies the full response-derived target package, rather
than a scalar dimension.  S2 distinguishes attraction from stability.  S5
excludes a target hard-coded into the family.  S6 excludes a non-\(3+1\)
predecessor being hidden by the quotient.  Thus the conclusion is dynamical
selection of the operational package, not compactification or a dimensional
estimator.

### Failure semantics

Failure or absence of S1, S3, S4, S5 or S6 is `inconclusive` about selection.
It is not a proof that no attractive target exists.  A selection claim is
excluded only when every interval-consistent microscopic dynamics candidate
has a genuine target obstruction.

The selection-level verdict therefore mirrors the repaired reconstruction
logic.

- If \(\mathfrak M_{\mathscr D}=\varnothing\), report microscopic
  class/data incompatibility separately.
- `certified within class` requires
  \(\mathsf T_{\rm sel}(M)\wedge\mathsf R_{\rm sel}(M,\mathscr D)\) for every

- `class-excluded` requires an exact or positively separated obstruction to
  \(\mathsf T_{\rm sel}(M)\) for every microscopic candidate.
- Every other nonempty-fiber case is `inconclusive`.

A missing scale interval, failed visibility proof or absent Lyapunov artifact
never enters the target-obstruction set by itself.
