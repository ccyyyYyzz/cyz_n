# 10. Exact source-boundary theorems

## Theorem 7 — amplitudes do not determine the clock

Fix a finite process (T,K,p_c). For a>0 define

```text

K^(a) = aK,
p_c^(a) = p_c.                                                  (68)
```

2. every conditional channel probability is unchanged;
3. encounter time rescales as τ^(a) distributed as τ^(1)/a;
4. accepted counts, pressure histories, first-entry probabilities, and strict residence generally change.

If the Duhamel pairing of this rate perturbation with a strict-residence event is nonzero, the corresponding E_m has nonzero derivative in a.

### Proof

Equation (2) gives H^(a)(dτ,dm)=a H^(1)(d(aτ),dm). The full generator contains K p_c, so changing a changes its finite-time semigroup unless the channel is dynamically null.

## Theorem 8 — one-mark marginals do not determine history

Let recollision mark z be zero or one with stationary marginal (1/2,1/2), and

```text

p_ann(1)=p,  0<p<1.                                           (69)
```

Compare iid redraw with a fully persistent mark. After n recollisions,

```text

S_persistent(n) = 1/2 + (1/2)(1−p)^n.                          (71)
```

They differ for n>=2. The one-mark marginal and conditional channel table are identical, but killed retention and leakage differ.

The partially persistent kernel

```text

```

interpolates continuously. Measuring only the impact marginal cannot determine ε.

## Theorem 9 — forward channels do not determine reverse production

Fix the forward clock, marks, and annihilation probabilities. Two positive reservoir density-of-states ratios


The primary inputs in Section 2 constrain conditional interaction probabilities on specified strata and parts of the geometry and thermodynamic drift. They do not jointly identify

