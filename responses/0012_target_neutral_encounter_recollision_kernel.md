## 5.2 Microcanonical reverse channel

Let \(\Omega_R(E_R)\) be the reservoir density of states, and let \(g_X(m)\) and

\[

\frac{g_X(m)\Omega_R(E_R^X)}

## Theorem 4 — detailed-balance reverse kernel

The finite reaction pair (35) is reversible with respect to

\[
\Pi(X)\propto g_X\Omega_R(E_R^X),
\tag{36}
\]

because

\[
\Pi(X)q_{X\to Y}=\Pi(Y)q_{Y\to X}.
\tag{37}

\[

=e^{-\beta\Delta E}
\left[1+O\!\left(\frac{\Delta E^2}{C_RT^2}\right)\right],
\tag{38}
\]

where the correction is a controlled thermodynamic expansion.

A free constant reverse edge is not used.  If the reservoir is not in a declared

# 10. Exact source-boundary theorems

## Theorem 7 — conditional amplitudes do not determine the clock

Fix one finite encounter process \((T,K,p_c)\).  For \(a>0\), define

\[
T^{(a)}=aT,
\qquad
K^{(a)}=aK,
\qquad
p_c^{(a)}=p_c.
\tag{77}
\]

Then:

1. the integrated encounter-mark distribution is unchanged;
2. every conditional channel probability \(p_c(x,m)\) is unchanged;
3. the encounter time rescales as
   
   \[
   \tau^{(a)}\overset d=\tau^{(1)}/a;
   \tag{78}
   \]
4. accepted event counts, geometry pressure histories, first-entry probabilities,
   and strict-residence probabilities generally change with \(a\).

If the Duhamel pairing of the rate perturbation with one strict-residence event is
nonzero, then the corresponding \(E_m(T,\Delta)\) has nonzero derivative in
\(a\).

### Proof

Equation (2) gives

\[
H^{(a)}(d\tau,dm)
=aH^{(1)}(d(a\tau),dm),
\]

which proves the first three statements.  The full winding--geometry generator
contains the accepted boundary rate \(Kp_c\), so changing \(a\) changes its
finite-time semigroup unless the reaction channel is dynamically null.  The
strict-residence derivative follows from Duhamel differentiation of (69).

Thus a crossing-conditioned amplitude cannot identify an attraction time or a
strict-residence gate without an absolute encounter clock.

## Theorem 8 — one-mark marginals do not determine recollision retention

Consider recollision marks \(z\in\{0,1\}\) with stationary marginal
\(\pi=(1/2,1/2)\), and conditional annihilation probabilities

\[
p_{\rm ann}(0)=0,
\qquad
p_{\rm ann}(1)=p,
\qquad 0<p<1.
\tag{79}
\]

Compare two target-neutral history laws with the same one-mark marginal.

1. **Independent redraw:** every recollision mark is redrawn from \(\pi\).
2. **Persistent history:** the initial mark has law \(\pi\) and is retained at all
   subsequent recollisions.

After \(n\) recollisions, the no-annihilation probabilities are

\[
S_{\rm iid}(n)=\left(1-\frac p2\right)^n,
\tag{80}
\]

\[
S_{\rm per}(n)=\frac12+\frac12(1-p)^n.
\tag{81}
\]

They differ for every \(n\ge2\).  Hence identical one-encounter mark marginals and
identical conditional channel probabilities can yield different killed retention
and leakage.

A partially persistent kernel

\[
R_\epsilon=(1-\epsilon)I+\epsilon\mathbf1\pi
\tag{82}
\]

interpolates continuously between the two cases.  Measuring only the marginal
impact distribution cannot determine \(\epsilon\).

## Theorem 9 — forward channels do not determine reverse production

Fix the forward encounter clock, mark law, and conditional annihilation
probability.  Let two reservoirs have different positive density-of-states ratios

\[
\frac{\Omega_R(E-\Delta E)}{\Omega_R(E)}.
\tag{83}
\]

Equation (35) gives different reverse creation rates while leaving the forward
source amplitude unchanged.  Therefore forward amplitudes alone do not determine
plateau retention or leakage in a regime where reverse production is allowed.

## Theorem 10 — exact source-boundary result

The primary-source inputs listed in Section 2 constrain conditional interaction
probabilities on specified source strata and parts of the geometry/thermodynamic
drift.  They do not jointly identify:

\[
T_{e,x},
\qquad
K_{e,x},
\qquad
R_{e,x},
\qquad
\Omega_R,
\qquad
\mu_0.
\tag{84}
\]

Even if \(p_c(x,m)\) is taken as known exactly, Theorems 7--9 produce
pre-classifier, target-neutral completions with different entrant strict
residence and killed leakage.  Consequently the source information does not
determine (75)--(76), and no source-only positive rank-three strict-residence
margin is certified.

This is an identifiability boundary, not a theorem that a future completed action
cannot select three.
